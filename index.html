About Us

CO₂ LOW is a South African non-profit pioneering practical, scalable solutions for food security, climate action, and community empowerment. We are a team of passionate change-makers, agricultural innovators, and community leaders committed to driving impact from the ground up.

We deliver holistic programs that bridge business, environmental, and social needs, working closely with rural farmers, youth, and industry partners. We offer carbon management consultancy combining international best practices with deep South African expertise.

Our Process

1. Assess

Detailed audit of current emissions using South African standards and methodologies.

2. Plan

Develop step-by-step roadmap aligned with South African climate policies and local regulations.

3. Act

Implement carbon reduction initiatives and offset remaining emissions through trusted South African and international projects.

From Waste to Wonder

The project transforms food waste into compost, animal feed, and biogas, creating a closed-loop system that reduces landfill waste and generates renewable energy.


Features include:

  • Market-guaranteed pricing
  • Training
  • Local job creation
  • Sustainable circular farming practices using certified compost

The project creates:

  • Stable income opportunities (R48,000–R84,000 per year per farmer on 0.25 ha)
  • Up to 480 local jobs
  • A replicable model balancing social equity, environmental stewardship, and commercial viability

Litter4Tokens

Litter4Tokens is a community-based initiative in Durban, South Africa, that tackles plastic waste and poverty by rewarding local collectors for gathering litter.


Key Achievements

  • Over 2.7 million bags of waste collected since 2015.
  • More than 2,940 tonnes diverted from landfills and waterways.
  • 982,000+ tokens issued, supporting access to basic needs for thousands of collectors.
  • Active network of 680+ waste pickers across 22 sites.

Why Support Litter4Tokens?
  • Reduces plastic pollution and landfill waste.
  • Empowers vulnerable communities with tangible economic benefits.
  • Offers a replicable and transparent model for sustainable environmental action.

South African Climate Context

South Africa faces unique climate challenges and opportunities. As one of the world's largest carbon emitters per capita, the country is transitioning toward net-zero emissions by 2050.

  • eThekwini Municipality's commitment to net-zero carbon buildings by 2030
  • South Africa's Climate Change Act and Nationally Determined Contributions
  • Local renewable energy and energy efficiency programs
  • C40 Cities climate action initiatives in Durban
